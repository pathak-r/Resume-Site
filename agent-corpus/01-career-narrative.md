# Career narrative

The resume says what; this doc says how and why. Write in first person, rough is fine.

## The two-minute story

> Prompt: how do you tell your career story in ~150 words? Engineering → support →
> PM → AI PM. What's the through-line?

I started my career as a technical support engineer for Smart3D at Hexagon AB — specifically its asset lifecycle division, Hexagon Asset Lifecycle Intelligence — in 2013 (right out of campus after my masters in mechanical engineering from NC State one of top US universities), the de facto #1 industry solution for enterprise engineering design, used by EPCs, Oil and Gas and Large Capital Projects and Infrastructure companies for the end to end design and operation of facilities. Power Plants,  Refineries, Fab Plants (Semiconductor etc) all such. I worked with such companies on some of the most complex and challenging engineering design problems for these companies. My mix of a background in mechanical engineering and software development skills that I picked up on the job was what this (and such companies want). I excelled at the role quickly and was very good at understanding customer requirements and either building or giving them the solution they wanted some way. This was noticed by management and I was offered a role in product management. I however wanted to continue to become technically better and therefore declined the offer at the time. I continued in the technical role for about 5 years total and then chose to move to product management and i accepted a PM role in the hyderabad office (relocated to india at the end of 2018). Here too i performed excellently modernizing smart3d to move it to the latest tech stack from vb6 to .net, 64 bit, i designed the new and improved UI / UX where i achieved a productivity gain of nearly 35% average on workflows and some workflows even 3X. Been integrating gen ai over the past 2-3 years into the product where we built a) natural language driven reports (no need to write SQL or use the API if you dont want to), natural language driven 3d workflows - you could talk to the system and say route a pipe connecting nozzle N5 and inlet I2 and it would just do it instead of you having to use the mouse. You could simply ask the system give me objects that meet criteria A, B, C, D and E it would give it you instead of you writing a SQL query. all of this very was well received. retained some top accounts and even managed to get some top accounts from the main competitor. got the voice of the customer award. quit in dec 2025 because my wife who works for microsoft got a great offer from abu dhabi and we had to relocate.

### PM, Hexagon (2018 — 2022)

- How the support→PM transition happened (who sponsored it, what you did to earn it):

i had been exceptional in product support, especially with custom application development where i worked with customers to build solutions to solve some really pressing problems on the smart 3d engineering design platform. One case was where i worked with a major chemicals owner-operator to build a solution that allowed data to easily flow to construction simulation software. another one would be where i built a custom reporting solution to determine spool sizing that allowed a top-tier global EPC to optimally ship piping assemblies from south korea to kazakhstan via small russian river systems. there was excellent customer feedback for my work, especially from the top EPC customers. Management of course was in the loop too. I was therefore asked by the director of product to come join them. this was when i had been in support for about 2.5 years. i declined at the time because i wanted to continue being in a technical role but then eventually accepted it in year 5.

- The "Voice of the Customer" award — what specifically was it for?

this was specifically for the modernization work, delivering targets before time and the improved workflow timings (35% average and some workflows even 3X), and productivity gains. 

- One story of taking an AI MVP from demo to product:

natural language retrieval of objects. i knew this is something customers want and will be a game changer. i therefore worked with the team to create a prototype where you could simply talk to the system and retrieve data / objects that you wanted. even get reports. exec management liked it, we productized it and it was a success. customers said this is exactly what we wanted. the customer advisory board that i had formed i'd frequently talk to them to keep things aligned.

### Support Analyst, Hexagon (2013 — 2018)

- The SDK automation that cut engineering effort 80% — what was it, for whom?

think of a repeatable module of a process plant. a slab which has equipment on it with a piping network that eventually fits in the plant. instead of having to manually create such repeatable units by hand each time i created a form where you could just change parameters and get the module. the smart 3d API was used for this (the pipe constructor API, the slab construction API and so on). it cut the time to roughly a fifth of what it took manually — about an 80% reduction.

- What those technical years give you as a PM today:

i am one of the rare pms who came from a dev / technical background so i know exactly what we are building and what it will take to build something. the dev team and i are on the same page and they dont have to spend time explaining stuff to me. it helps me take decisions very effectively. i can visualise and think through implementations in my head. i can understand technical limitations and why something cannot be built. i also know if something is easy and cheap to build and so on. none of this is just intuition , i can actually think through these things. i can build stuff nearly all the way when needed (especially with AI) that helps getting early feedback without tying up dev resources and so on.
